Aerospace and Aviation

Cabin crew or Flight attendant

Earn certifications After completing your 12th with 50% marks, you may pursue a certification programme to become a flight attendant. Admissions to the certification programme may involve an interview and examination to determine your communication abilities and job aptitude.

  • Get a diploma The diploma courses for a flight attendant is a one-year programme where you may require passing an examination to get admission. The curriculum is like that of a certification programme. Along with the certification course's topics, you can learn leadership, inter-departmental communication, technical training on aircraft functioning and safety procedures.
  • Consider pursuing an undergraduate degree Having a bachelor's degree is not generally mandatory for the flight attendant job. You can consider pursuing a bachelor's degree in language, hospitality, communication or public relations to enhance your knowledge and communication abilities. Having a degree in foreign languages can provide you with opportunities to work with international airlines.
  • Gain relevant work experience Try getting some experience in the customer service job roles during your education. It may help you develop the skills to interact with customers that are very important for the flight attendant job role. You may apply for a part-time or full-time role as receptionist, server or customer relationship executive.
  • Apply for a job at an airline Search for job openings of flight attendant positions, customise your resume for the specific airline and submit your application.
